Bistro-Style Steak

Description

Chef John's steak frites recipe features a pan-seared and pan-sauced steak, just like you'd get at a French bistro. If you always grill steak, this will be a game-changer.
This method delivers a perfectly prepared, perfectly seasoned steak, and can be used with many different cuts beyond New York strip.

Ingredients

  • 1 (12-ounce) New York strip steak
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1/4 cup sliced shallots
  • 1/4 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup red wine
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon cold butter

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  2. Season steak with salt and black pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a cast iron skillet over high heat until it just begins to smoke.
  4. Place steak in the hot skillet and cook until it begins to firm and is reddish-pink and juicy in the center, about 4 minutes per side.
  5. Transfer steak to a plate and cover with aluminum foil.
  6. Reduce heat to medium and melt 1 tablespoon butter in the same skillet.
  7. Cook and stir shallots and mushrooms in the melted butter until mushrooms are browned, 5 to 7 minutes.
  8. Pour red wine into skillet and bring to a boil while scraping the browned bits of food off of the bottom of the skillet with a wooden spoon.
  9. Stir beef broth into wine mixture and bring to a simmer.
  10. Whisk Dijon mustard into sauce and simmer until sauce is slightly reduced, about 5 minutes.
  11. Remove skillet from heat and stir parsley and cold butter into sauce until butter is melted.
  12. Return steak to skillet and spoon sauce over steak.
  13. Place skillet in the preheated oven and cook until steak is cooked to your desired degree of doneness, 5 to 10 minutes.
  14. Remove skillet from oven and let steak rest for 5 minutes before slicing.
